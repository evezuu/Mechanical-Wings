## Project Description
This project is a wearable set of motorized animatronic wings. It has a wearable backpack-mounted frame, and it's designed to open and close using two synchorized 12V linear actuators that are controlled by a wireless remote. The frame is constructed from lightweight PVC tubing and ABS plastic, which makes it durable and also light enough for me to have it on my back and carry it around. It uses a rechargeable 12V battery to power the whole thing.

## Electrical System

The electrical system powers and controls the movement of the animatronic wings. It consists of a rechargeable 12V battery, a wireless two-channel motor controller, and two 12V linear actuators.

The battery provides power to the motor controller, which receives commands from the wireless remote and controls the direction of the actuators. Since the actuators use DC motors, the direction of movement is controlled by reversing the polarity of the voltage. Sending power in one direction causes the actuators to extend, while reversing the polarity causes them to retract.

Both actuators are connected in parallel so that the wings receive the same control signals and move together. This allows the wings to flare and retract at the same time. 

## Mechanical System
The mechanical system converts the linear movement of the actuators into the rotational movement needed to open and close the wings.

Each wing is made from multiple connected sections joined through pivot points. These joints use bolts, washers, and lock nuts to allow smooth rotation while maintaining structural stability. The linear actuators connect to the wing linkage and push or pull the mechanism to create movement. As the actuators extend, the linkage rotates the wing sections outward. As they retract, the wings fold back into their resting position.

The main frame is constructed from PVC tubing because it is lightweight, affordable, durable, and easy to modify. The entire mechanism is mounted onto an ABS plastic backing plate shaped to fit the user's body. This plate functions like a backpack frame, distributing the weight across the shoulders and upper back instead of concentrating the load in one area.

## How the wings will be put together
The build will begin by shaping and preparing the ABS mounting plate, which will support the actuators and the rest of the wing mechanism. The PVC wing frames will then be assembled by drilling holes into them and adding bolts, washers, and lock nuts to secure them together. This creates the structure needed for the wings to fold and extend.

After the frame is assembled, the linear actuators will be placed on the lowest left and right pipe of the actuator, allowing them to provide the force for the wings to flare out and retract. The placement of these connections will be adjusted during testing to ensure both wings move smoothly and evenly.

The electrical components will then be mounted onto the frame and connected to the actuators. Straps will be added to the ABS mounting plate to keep them in place. The wireless controller will allow the wings to be operated remotely.

Once the mechanical and electrical systems are working correctly, the outer wing covering and decorative elements will be added. A lot of feathers will be attached onto my frame using wire. These will be tested carefully since additional weight can affect the movement and performance of the mechanism.
